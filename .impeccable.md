## Design Context

### Users
High-net-worth UK and international investors researching residential property investment opportunities. They arrive informed, expect substance over style, and make decisions based on trust, track record, and momentum. Also: clients seeking bespoke portfolio building — looking for a credible, ambitious partner, not just a middleman.

**Job to be done:** Assess Luli Properties as a serious, forward-moving investment firm. Understand the portfolio and services. Make contact.

**Context:** Primarily desktop, considered research sessions. They've seen a dozen generic property sites. This one needs to feel like a different category entirely — sharper, more ambitious, more alive.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Modern. Ambitious. Sharp.

**Emotional goal:** Within 5 seconds, the visitor should feel: "This firm is going places. I want to be associated with it." Not intimidating luxury — confident momentum. The energy of a firm that knows exactly where it's headed.

**What it is NOT:** Not precious. Not estate-agent generic. Not loud SaaS startup. Not glassmorphism. Not boucing animations. Not "luxury print magazine for its own sake." Not rounded card grids.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Cinematic editorial with contemporary ambition. OliviaHarperHomes (oliviaharperhomes.com) is the primary reference — scroll-driven narrative, character-split text, restrained warm palette, stacking cards, custom cursor. FindRealEstate (findrealestate.com) is the professional baseline — clarity, structure, modern sans-serif legibility.

**Layout:** Editorial/magazine structure — asymmetric, extreme typographic scale, full-bleed sections, photography as architecture. Left-aligned compositions with deliberate tension. Not centered. Not symmetric. Not a grid of rounded cards.

**Dark sections:** Strategic use mid-page encouraged. One to two per page in deep sage (`#2E4330`) for cinematic rhythm. Light sections breathe more when anchored by dark ones.

**Anti-references:** Generic estate agent CTA buttons. Property portal grid clutter. Glassmorphism or gradient text. Bounce/elastic animations. Centered everything. Repeating card grids. Anything that looks like a prop-tech startup.

**Copy style:** No em dashes anywhere. Not in UI copy, headings, labels, or code comments. Use a comma, period, or rewrite instead.

**Theme:** Light dominant. Warm off-white base (`#FAF8F5`). Deep sage for dramatic full-section moments. Sage and champagne gold as precise, restrained accents.

### Typography
- **Display (H1–H3, hero):** Cormorant Garamond — `font-display`. Extreme scale. H1: `clamp(3.25rem, 7.5vw, 7.5rem)`. Italic on key editorial phrases.
- **Body / UI:** DM Sans — `font-body`. Clean, geometric, sharp. Nav, buttons, labels, body copy.
- **Weight contrast:** Heavy/bold Cormorant headlines against light DM Sans body — 4x–6x scale ratio minimum.
- **Ghost type:** Oversized numbers or initials as absolute-positioned background furniture behind grids and section headers.
- **Structural numbering:** Section numbers (01, 02...) and property list numbers as deliberate typographic elements.

### Palette

| `gold-400` | `#C9A24A` | Secondary CTAs, thin rules, structural lines |
| `neutral-50` | `#FAF8F5` | Page background (warm off-white) |

| `sage-700` | `#2E4330` | Footer, strategic dark sections |
| `white` | `#FFFFFF` | Cards, modals |

Never pure gray or pure black. Always tinted warm.

### Signature Interactions (priority order)
1. **Character-split text animations** — Headlines animate in word-by-word or letter-by-letter on page load and scroll reveal. Staggered, deliberate, never scattered. Use Framer Motion `motion.span` with `AnimatePresence`.
2. **Video background hero** — Full-screen atmospheric video (Unsplash/pexels embed or MP4) behind the hero section. Text overlaid with subtle veil. Pauses on `prefers-reduced-motion`.
3. **Stacking scroll cards** — Portfolio cards layer and scale based on scroll position, creating depth. Cards arrive stacked, each revealing as the previous settles.
4. **Numbered hover list** — Portfolio properties listed as large uppercase numbered rows. Hovering reveals a floating photo preview tracking the cursor.

### Motion Principles
- Orchestrated entrances — things arrive in deliberate sequence, never scattered
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.19, 1, 0.22, 1)` (expo out) for structural moves. `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)` for content reveals
- Duration: 700–1200ms entrances, 300ms max micro-interactions
- **Never** bounce or elastic. **Never** abrupt cuts. **Always** `prefers-reduced-motion` respected — jump to final state instantly, never disable functionality
- Exit animations are ~75% of enter duration
- Stagger delays: 80–120ms between related elements

### Design Principles
1. **Make them stop** — every section has one element that earns a moment of attention. One hero moment per section, not many competing ones.
2. **Momentum through proportion** — dramatic type scale and asymmetry do more than any visual effect. Structure IS the design.
3. **Dark sections are editorial punctuation** — one or two per page for cinematic rhythm. They make everything around them breathe.
4. **Everything converges on contact** — every page, section, and CTA arc toward `/contact`. Never let the visitor reach a dead end.
5. **Interactions reveal craft** — cursor, stacking cards, character splits — each feels inevitable, purposeful, not decorative.
6. **Ambitious, not precious** — this is a firm going places. The design should feel like momentum, not museum curation.

### Quality Bar
- **Homepage:** Flagship. Video hero, character-split headline, stacking portfolio preview, all signature interactions present.
- **Portfolio page:** The numbered hover list is a flagship interaction — implement fully.
- **Inner pages (/about, /services, /contact):** Clean, on-brand, functional. Same type and palette. Scroll reveals apply, lighter interaction load.

### Accessibility
WCAG 2.1 AA. Min 4.5:1 body contrast, 3:1 large text. Keyboard navigable. Custom cursor is additive (default cursor still functional). `prefers-reduced-motion`: jump to final state instantly, never disable functionality. Video hero: paused, no autoplay audio.
