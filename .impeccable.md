## Design Context

### Users
High-net-worth UK and international investors researching residential property investment opportunities. They arrive informed, expect substance over style, and make decisions based on trust and track record. Also: clients seeking bespoke property portfolio building — slightly less experienced, looking for a trustworthy guide.

**Job to be done:** Assess Luli Properties as a credible investment partner, understand the portfolio and services, then make contact.

**Context:** Primarily desktop browsing, during considered research sessions. Not impulse visits. They've seen a dozen generic property sites — this one needs to stop them.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Cinematic. Precise. Unforgettable.

**Emotional goal:** Sharp intake of breath on first load — "who made this?" — followed by immediate trust. The site signals Luli operates at a different level. Every element feels considered, every interaction purposeful. Not a developer portfolio. A design agency that's been doing this for 20 years.

**What it is NOT:** Not bouncy, not generic SaaS, not estate agent, not prop-tech startup.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Cinematic Editorial — luxury print magazine brought to life. Architectural use of space, extreme typographic scale, drama through proportion rather than colour. Left-aligned, asymmetric, structurally intentional.

**Reference style** (from provided screenshots — Horizon Estate / Kontako aesthetic):
- Large heavy headlines left-aligned, subtext in a right column
- Full-bleed architectural photography as structural element
- Numbered everything: properties, sections, gallery items (01, 02, 03...)
- Ghost/watermark type (large initials or numbers) behind photo grids as typographic furniture
- Dark full-bleed sections with dramatic headline overlaid on photography
- Minimal colour — photography and structure carry the weight
- Paired CTAs: filled primary + outline secondary

**Standout interaction pattern (must implement):**
The numbered hover list — property names listed as large uppercase rows with numbers (01, 02, 03...). On hover, a floating photo preview appears beside the active row. Extremely elegant, very editorial, nothing like a standard property grid.

**Dark sections:** Strategic use mid-page is permitted and encouraged. One or two sections in deep sage (`#2E4330`) or near-black with text/photo overlap creates cinematic depth and makes surrounding light sections breathe more.

**Anti-references:** No estate agent red/blue CTAs. No property portal grid clutter. No glassmorphism or neon gradients. No centered everything. No repeating rounded cards. No developer-portfolio parallax tricks.

**Theme:** Light mode dominant. Warm off-white base (#FAF8F5). Deep sage for dramatic full-section moments. Sage and champagne gold as precise accents only.

### Typography
- **Display / Headings:** Cormorant Garamond — editorial serif, prestigious. H1–H3, hero text, pull quotes. Italic for emphasis on key phrases.
- **Body / UI:** DM Sans — clean geometric humanist. Body copy, labels, nav, buttons, form fields.
- **Scale:** Never timid. Headline should feel like it could bleed off the screen. `clamp(3.25rem, 7.5vw, 7.5rem)` for H1. 4x–6x ratio between headline and label text.
- **Ghost type:** Large project numbers or initials placed as absolute-positioned background furniture behind photo grids and section headers.
- **Structural numbering:** Section numbers (01, 02...) and property list numbers (01, 02, 03, 04) as deliberate typographic elements, not decoration.

### Palette
- **Sage (primary accent):** `#4E7050` — CTAs, location tags, icon accents, section tints
- **Gold/Champagne (secondary accent):** `#C9A24A` — secondary CTAs, thin rules, structural gold lines
- **Base:** `#FAF8F5` (warm off-white page bg), `#FFFFFF` (cards), `#F2EDE6` (section tints)
- **Dark:** `#2E4330` (deep sage) — footer, strategic dark sections mid-page
- **Tinted neutrals only:** Never pure gray or pure black. Always use the warm palette.

### Motion & Interactions
**Signature elements already built:**
- Custom cursor: dot (exact) + spring-lagged ring with HOVER/DRAG states. Desktop-only.
- Horizontal drag carousel: portfolio cards with `useMotionValue`, progress bar, `isDragging` link guard.
- Phrase-by-phrase cinematic hero entrances with deliberate stagger.
- Full-viewport-width gold rules that draw left→right after headings settle.

**To build (derived from reference):**
- **Numbered hover list** for portfolio page — large uppercase property names, numbered rows, floating photo preview on hover (uses `useMotionValue` for photo position tracking)
- **Staggered overlapping photo grid** — 4 photos with 01–04 numbering, offset positions, ghost watermark behind
- **Scroll-triggered line reveals** — text lines or rules that draw/clip in as they enter viewport
- **Parallax on section imagery** — offset scroll rate on hero/section photos

**Motion principles:**
- Orchestrated entrances, not scatter reveals. Sequence matters — things arrive in deliberate order.
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.19, 1, 0.22, 1)` (expo out) for structural. `cubic-bezier(0.25, 0.46, 0.45, 0.94)` for content.
- Duration: 700–1200ms for entrances. 300ms max for micro-interactions.
- NEVER bounce or elastic easing. NEVER abrupt.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` always respected — jump to final state, no animation.

### Design Principles
1. **Make them stop scrolling** — every section has one element that earns a moment of attention
2. **Drama through proportion, not decoration** — extreme scale and asymmetry over visual effects
3. **Dark sections are editorial punctuation** — use one or two per page for cinematic rhythm
4. **Everything converges on contact** — every page, section, and CTA arcs toward `/contact`
5. **Interactions reveal craft** — the numbered hover list, drag carousel, custom cursor — each feels inevitable, not decorative
6. **Structure over decoration** — numbered systems, ghost type, full-bleed rules give structure its own visual weight

### Quality Bar
- **Homepage:** Flagship. Full Cinematic Editorial treatment. Every section has a signature moment.
- **Portfolio page:** The numbered hover list is a flagship interaction — implement it fully.
- **Inner pages (/about, /services, /contact):** Clean, on-brand, functional. Same typography and palette. Scroll reveals apply, lighter interaction load.

### Accessibility
WCAG 2.1 AA. Min 4.5:1 body contrast, 3:1 large text. Keyboard navigable. Custom cursor is additive (default cursor still functional). `prefers-reduced-motion` always respected — jump to final state, never disable functionality.
